About MEWA Regulatory Sandbox

MEWA is launching a regulatory sandbox to support innovative technologies in the critical sectors of Environment, Water and Agriculture. The regulatory sandbox program will provide controlled test environments in which innovative technologies and business models can be tried out under the supervision of the regulator. By participating in MEWAâ€™s regulatory sandbox, innovators can tackle regulatory challenges head-on and address barriers to innovation

Objectives of the MEWA Regulatory Sandbox

Address regulatory barriers facing innovators

Ensure regulations are adaptive to

innovation trends & disruptions

Eligibility

Startups and firms innovating in the environment, water, or agriculture sectors are encouraged to apply

Innovative solutions

Firms looking to deploy innovative solutions in the environment, water and agriculture sectors aligned with MEWA regulatory sandbox's core themes

Economic and social benefit

Innovators offering solutions with clear economic and social contributions to the KSA economy

Capability readiness

Innovators with the resources and expertise to conduct regulatory sandbox testiness and demonstrate readiness to deploy solution in the market

Clarity on regulatory barriers

Innovators that have a clear and specifically defined regulatory barriers that are applicable to the regulatory sandbox

Clear action plan

Innovators with a clear scope, timeline, and defined testing conditions for regulatory barriers

Clear exit plan

Innovators with a proposal to conduct a test with defined success parameters and clear outcomes following potential test completion

Ability to implement successful outcomes

Innovators with a proposed pilot that has been successfully implemented in other countries or if established standards and local regulatory tools are readily available

Core Themes

MEWA Regulatory Sandbox invites innovative technologies focused on core themes in the Environment, Water, and Agricultural sectors

Environment

  • Smart irrigation

  • Grazing land management

  • Waste treatment

  • Ecosystem monitoring

  • Weather and climate forecasting

Water

  • Advanced RO

  • Adaptive technologies for wastewater reuse

  • Smart leakage management

  • Detect, locate, and mitigate leaks in water distribution systems

  • Innovative water consumption in farming

Agriculture

  • Irrigation and water management

  • Integrated aquaculture management

  • Protected agriculture and controlled environment

  • Food loss and waste

  • Drones and satellites
